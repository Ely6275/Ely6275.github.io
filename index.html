About me

A little background

I'm a freshman at Duke University studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, with roots in robotics and programming that go back to high school — where I competed in VEX Robotics and led both my school's math and coding clubs.

I'm drawn to problems that live at the intersection of hardware and software, where the math and the physical thing you're building have to work together. I learn by doing and I like working with others, whether that's teaching Arduino to classmates or debugging a robot at midnight. What keeps me going is the belief that intelligent systems can meaningfully change how the world works.
