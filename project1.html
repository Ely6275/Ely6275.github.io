Vision Controlled Prosthetic Hand

A low-cost 3D-printed prosthetic hand teleoperated by real-time computer vision: MediaPipe hand tracking converts 21 landmarks per frame into servo angles, mirroring the user's hand on physical hardware with no gloves or flex sensors.

COMPUTER VISION

OpenCV captures the live feed frame by frame; MediaPipe's hand landmark model returns XYZ coordinates for 21 landmarks in real time — fingertip and base knuckle extracted per finger.

CONTROL MAPPING

Euclidean tip-to-knuckle distance maps linearly to servo angle, with a tuned gain and explicit edge-case handling for fully closed fingers and the thumb's off-axis motion.

HARDWARE

Open-source InMoov 3D-printed hand driven by five MG996R servos on an Arduino Nano. Tendon actuation — fishing line through the finger joints — keeps the build cheap with realistic range of motion.

SOFTWARE DESIGN

Three-module object-oriented architecture with PyFirmata2 handling Python-to-Arduino serial — servo angles stream over USB with no custom firmware required.

02 — CONTROL LAW

From Pixels to Servo Angles

Each finger's openness is measured as the Euclidean pixel distance between its fingertip and base knuckle landmarks:

d = √((x₁ − x₂)² + (y₁ − y₂)²)

A longer distance means an extended finger; shorter means curling closed. The distance maps to a servo angle through a linear gain — after testing, a multiplier of 40 produced a full, natural range of motion:

θ = 40d
  • Closed-fist edge case: when a finger fully closes, the tip landmark drops below the knuckle in screen coordinates. A conditional catches the Y-inversion and drives the servo straight to 180°, eliminating jitter at the extremes.
  • Thumb handling: the thumb moves side-to-side rather than curling, so its distance is computed from a right triangle built on the tip and base coordinates instead of a straight line.

03 — ARCHITECTURE

Software Structure

  • handControl.py — main loop: captures frames, runs landmark detection, computes distances, and writes angles to all five servos.
  • Finger.py — a Finger object holding tip/base coordinates and its servo, with an isThumb flag for the alternate distance calculation.
  • Servo_Utils.py — a Servo class wrapping PyFirmata2's serial connection with a clean write(angle) interface per motor.

06 — ROADMAP

Known Limits & Next Iterations

Building this exposed the fundamental constraints of camera-only control — each one maps to a concrete next step:

  • Per-user calibration: the fixed distance-to-angle gain assumes one hand geometry. Next version normalizes distances against each user's finger proportions.
  • Stereo depth: a single camera can't separate finger motion from the whole hand moving toward the lens, causing false actuation. A stereo camera pair would recover true depth.
  • EMG + machine learning: the long-term direction is dropping the camera entirely — training a model on labeled EMG muscle signals to predict intended gestures, enabling far more natural prosthetic control.
